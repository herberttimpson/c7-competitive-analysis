Key Findings — Whole/Half Market

  • C7 is priced above market avg on 4 of 5 products — this is a premium brand
  • Black Angus: +20% above whole/half avg. Premium requires storytelling (prior comp to $12.63 retail avg was wrong — that included individual cuts)
  • F1 Cross: +7% above market. Most defensible — Chappell Hill at $14.73–16.41 brackets C7 squarely
  • Full Blood Wagyu: +11%. Wilders at $15 is the only lower-priced fullblood whole/half seller
  • Kurobuta: +11% above Kurobuta-labeled whole/half avg ($9 avg). Name change moves C7 from "+25% above generic Berkshire" to a defensible +11% within the Kurobuta peer set
  • Lamb: raised to $15 whole / $16 half — now −3% from market avg, essentially at market. Auction spread improved from +$0.19 to +$0.85/lb

Action Items

  • Lamb: Locked at $15 whole / $16 half. +$92/animal over prior pricing. At market mid — hold this price.
  • Black Angus: +20% premium needs "why" on every product page — breed, feed, ranch, elevation.
  • Kurobuta: Name change validates current $10/$11 pricing — no need to raise. "Kurobuta · 100% Full Blood Berkshire" in every headline. Individual cut channel ($14+/lb) is the next revenue lever.
  • F1 Cross: Chappell Hill conversion data confirms $15 whole is well within market range — hold price.
  • Full Blood Wagyu: Half at $22 ($5,544) is the strongest revenue per-half in the lineup — prioritize half sales.
  • All: Shipping (~$0.50–1.00/lb average) adds to buyer's true cost — should appear in checkout estimates.
